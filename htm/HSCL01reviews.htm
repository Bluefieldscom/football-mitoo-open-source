Weekend Review for the 27th April 2002

For the second successive season the Premier Division title will be decided by the last match of the season. Sun Postal Sports gained the victory they needed to return to top spot two points ahead of reigning champions Oxhey Jets who play their final match on Monday. Sun left it desperately late at lowly Elliott Star before recording a 1-0 away victory with an 89th minute goal. Now, if Oxhey win their last game at home to Wormley Rovers on Monday night they’ll retain their title. Any other result and Sun will be crowned champions. Sandridge Rovers beat Croxley Guild 4-1 to ensure Guild finish second bottom whilst Rovers climb to sixth.

Thursday evening’s Chairman’s Cup Final will be between Bedmond Sports and Wormley Rovers – the game to be staged by Bedmond. That’s after Bedmond overcame Chipperfield Corinthians 2-1 in one semi whilst Wormley Rovers beat surprise packets Standon & Puckeridge 3-0 in the other semi. Kick off at Toms Lane is scheduled for 6.30pm.

In Division One Champions Hadley finished with a 1-1 home draw against North Mymms whilst Bushey Rangers celebrated their runners up finish with a 6-1 win at Benington.

The Reserve Cup Final, played at Lemsford, saw League status adhered to with Reserve Division One Champions Oxhey Jets beating Reserve Division Two Champions London Lions 5-0. In fairness to Lions they weren’t five goals worst than the Jets and for spells matched their opponents for industry. An early goal for Jets didn’t help their cause but the score remained 1-0 at the interval. Lions enjoyed their best spell early in the second period but never really looked like scoring. In the final 15 minutes the floodgates opened and once Oxhey scored their second goal the tie was effectively over. Three more goals followed as the referee extended Lions misery with six or so minutes of injury time. Disappointment though for Jets in this period as they had a player sent off. So the Oxhey Jets Reserve side have now completed a League and cup double and now wait to see if the First team can emulate them – the next nine days will reveal the answer.

 

Weekend Review for the 20th April 2002

The Premier Division title battle now looks certain to go to the final game as Oxhey Jets caught up on their backlog of League fixtures with a 5-2 victory at Elliott Star whom they beat 11 days ago in the Herts Senior Centenary Cup Final. The three points from the game added to the three picked up in midweek when they beat fourth placed Bovingdon 3-1 means they now have 51 points just two behind leaders Sun Postal Sports. Jets have two games left whilst Sun have just one. At the other end of the table, Hatfield Town are confirmed in bottom spot following a 3-2 home defeat at the hands of second bottom Croxley Guild. Guild could yet climb out of the bottom three if they can win their final matches. Metropolitan Police Bushey can’t escape a bottom three placing though as they lost their crucial final match 1-0 at St Peters who ensured their own safety with the three points. Elliott Star still need a point to be safe. They lost 2-0 in midweek at Wormley Rovers and then today, as mentioned earlier. The final Premier Division game of the day saw Cuffley record a 2-0 home win over Sandridge Rovers. The result gives Cuffley a mid-table berth after a season of internal turmoil.

In Division One Hadley confirmed the Championship with a 6-0 drubbing of strugglers Benington. That result along with Old Parmiterians 3-0 victory at second placed Bushey Rangers means the Londoners can’t be caught. Parmiters could yet finish runners up though if they win their last match and Rangers fail to win theirs.

The Chairman’s Cup semi-finals were confirmed as Wormley Rovers beat The Cheshunt Club 6-1 to win Group C and Standon & Puckeridge, the competitions surprise side, beat Welwyn 3-2 to finish level on points with Wormley Rovers and top runners-up from the three groups.

Bedmond Sports now entertain Chipperfield Corinthians in one semi whilst Wormley Rovers entertain Standon & Puckeridge in the other. Both games take place next weekend with the final scheduled for the following Thursday evening.

Weekend Review for the 13th April 2002

Both main League titles continue to remain up for grabs following this weekends results.

In the Premier Division it’s a two horse race with Bovingdon bowing out of the battle following a 3-2 defeat at Sandridge Rovers. Sun Postal Sports remain favourites following their 4-0 victory at relegation haunted Metropolitan Police Bushey. Oxhey Jets are now the only team who can overhaul them and they kept their hopes alive with a 3-0 win at Cuffley. At the bottom, Elliott Star appear to have saved themselves following a 2-1 victory at fellow strugglers Croxley Guild they are now three points clear of the bottom three and have a better goal difference. St Peters are still in the mire though following a 2-1 defeat at relegated Hatfield Town. Saints with 27 points are just a point above third bottom Met. Police.

Only one match in Division One but a hugely significant one. Hadley lost their unbeaten home record to second placed Bushey Rangers and although they remain top with a game in hand, the 3-1 victory moves Rangers to within a point.

In the Chairman’s Cup, Bedmond clinched Group A after a goalless draw at Old Parmiterians. Lemsford are runners-up in the group following a 5-0 victory over North Mymms. Chipperfield Corinthians won Group B without a ball being kicked, Sarratt unable to field a team against their near neighbours. Evergreen moved into second spot with a 3-0 home victory over Mill End Sports. Group C remains open. Wormley Rovers top the group on goal difference following an 8-0 thrashing of Welwyn but Standon & Puckeridge have the same points total after their 3-2 victory at The Cheshunt Club.

Finally, double congratulations to Oxhey Jets. On Tuesday evening at Letchworth they recorded their first ever success in the Herts Senior Centenary Trophy Final, beating fellow County League side Elliott Star in a hugely entertaining match which ended 4-3. Then on Wednesday evening their Reserves retained the Reserve Division One title following a 3-0 home win over challengers Bedmond Sports.

Weekend Review for the 6th April 2002

The battle for the Premier Division crown and to avoid relegation continues to rage at either end of the table. Sun Postal Sports maintained the League leadership with a comfortable 3-0 home win over one time challengers London Lions. Lions closed their campaign somewhat tamely and having occupied a top three spot for virtually the whole season end in fifth place. Bovingdon maintained the pressure on Sun with a 4-0 away victory at relegation haunted Croxley Guild. Still mathematically in the hunt are reigning champions Oxhey Jets. They scored a useful 2-0 away success at St Peters but are still eight points off the pace with three matches in hand. Cup commitments may yet decide their fate. Defeat for St Peters leaves them nervously looking over their shoulders as they now sit just one point above the relegation zone. Cuffley moved a little way clear of the relegation fight with a 4-1 home win over Hatfield Town whose fate is now sealed. The major game at the bottom saw Elliott Star record a vital 4-1 win at Metropolitan Police Bushey. The two clubs are now level on points but swap places on goal difference with Star out of the bottom three as they look ahead to Tuesday evenings Herts Centenary Trophy Final against Oxhey Jets at Letchworth. In a mid table clash, Wormley Rovers recorded a creditable 3-1 away win at Sandridge Rovers.

Only two matches in Division One. Hadley improved their championship prospects with a 1-0 home win over Evergreen whilst at the foot of the table Benington recorded a 4-2 home success against Sarratt. Sarratt remain bottom whilst Benington climb to fourth bottom.

In The Chairman’s Cup, Bedmond Sports retained their 100% record in Group A with a hard fought 2-1 home win over Division One outfit North Mymms. Lemsford moved into second spot in the Group with a 1-0 home victory against Old Parmiterians. Chipperfield Corinthians maintained a 100% record in Group B following a close 1-0 success at Mill End Sports. In Group C, Welwyn are the surprise leaders following a 6-1 thrashing of The Cheshunt Club.

Weekend Review for the 30th March 2002

Sun Postal Sports maintained their hopes of a Premier Division and Aubrey Cup double with a hard fought point at relegation threatened Cuffley – the result 1-1. Bedmond Sports & Social demonstrated they haven’t yet given up on the title with an impressive 2-0 win at reigning champions Oxhey Jets. The result puts Bedmond level on points with Sun but having played two games more. Jets are now eight points behind the leaders but have two games in hand. At the other end of the table, Croxley Guild picked up a valuable three points with a 2-0 home win against St Peters. Guild remain second from bottom but are now only five points adrift of safety and have two games in hand. That safe spot is occupied by Metropolitan Police Bushey who went down 2-1 at Sandridge Rovers in the battle of the beaten Aubrey Cup semi-finalists.

In Division One Hadley regained top spot with a 6-0 thrashing of The Cheshunt Club at The Whitewebbs Centre. Bushey Rangers also leapfrogged morning leaders Old Parmiterians thanks to a 3-2 home win against Kimpton Rovers. Congratulations to Benington. As April looms they finally recorded their first home win of the season, 2-1 over Welwyn to remain above their opponents and bottom place Sarratt.

In the Chairman’s Cup, Premier Division Chipperfield Corinthians narrowly edged out Division One side Evergreen 2-1. Standon & Puckeridge achieved perhaps the best result of the afternoon holding their Premier Division opponents 2-2. Elsewhere North Mymms and Old Parmiterians also drew 2-2 whilst Mill End Sports hammered Sarratt 9-0.

The Reserve Cup Final will be between London Lions and Oxhey Jets. Reserve Division Two highfliers London Lions overcame Reserve Division One strugglers Wormley Rovers 4-2. A much closer encounter at Bovingdon though where Oxhey Jets and the home side were goalless at the end of 90 minutes normal time and 30 minutes extra time. In the penalty shoot out Jets eventually edged through 8-7.

Weekend Review for the 23rd March 2002

Bank Holiday May Day Monday will see an all Watford Aubrey Cup Final clash at Harefield between Oxhey Jets and Sun Postal Sports.

After crushing Sandridge Rovers in the League last week, Oxhey Jets found the Rovers much tougher opposition in this afternoon’s Cup Semi-Final clash. Jets won through in the end by 2-1 to experience their first ever Aubrey Cup Final.

By comparison Sun Postal Sports could almost claim rights to the trophy having been finalists on no fewer than eight occasions. Their 2-0 home victory over Metropolitan Police Bushey followed the form book and denied the Police a second final appearance.

In the League, Bovingdon increased the pressure on the title favourites with a 3-0 home win over relegation threatened Croxley Guild. Elliott Star ended London Lions slim hopes of the title with a vital 1-0 home victory which keeps alive their chances of avoiding the drop although they remain three points below the safety line. Hatfield Town appear to be destined for a rapid return to Division One as they lost 5-1 at home against Chipperfield Corinthians. The days remaining clash saw Wormley Rovers win 2-0 at St Peters in a mid-table game.

In Division One Old Parmiterians took over top spot following a 1-0 home win against Standon & Puckeridge. Hadley still remain favourites to take the title though and they recorded a 6-0 home win over Mill End Sports to move into second spot and with four games in hand on the leaders. Morning leaders Bushey Rangers dropped to third following the postponement of their match at Benington. Evergreen beat The Cheshunt Club 3-0 in a mid-table battle.

The first match in the Chairman’s Cup took place with Premier Division Bedmond Sports picking up three points at Division One Lemsford. The final score, 3-1.

Weekend Review for the 16th March 2002

Another twist in the battle for the Premier Division title as Bedmond fell to a surprise defeat at relegation threatened Cuffley, 1-0, whilst Sun Postal Sports took three valuable home points with a 2-0 win over St Peters who have upset several of the top sides in recent weeks. Sun now return to the top with a two point advantage over Bedmond and with two games in hand. London Lions challenge seems to have died as they lost 4-0 at home against Bovingdon who leapfrog them to move into third spot. Don’t rule out reigning Champions Oxhey Jets holding on to their title though as they crushed Sandridge Rovers 4-0 in a rehearsal for next weekends Aubrey Cup semi-final encounter. The situation at the bottom now looks dire for the bottom three. Hatfield Town went down 5-3 at Metropolitan Police Bushey and remain rooted to the bottom. Croxley Guild lost 3-1 at home against Wormley Rovers and, despite having games in hand are only a point ahead of Hatfield. Elliott Star, Centenary Trophy Finalists, lost 3-1 at Chipperfield Corinthians and are now two points ahead of Guild but six points away from safety.

In Division One Bushey Rangers moved two points clear of second placed Hadley thanks to a 0-0 draw at Standon & Puckeridge. Hadley yet again postponed on their Brickfield Lane ground and now have three matches in hand on Rangers. Old Parmiterians closed to within two points of the second placed team with a 2-0 home success over Sarratt. The top three have now mathematically earned promotion. The afternoon’s remaining game saw end-of-season form side, Kimpton Rovers, beat Mill End Sports 5-3. Earlier Benington’s clash with The Cheshunt Club also fell victim to the damp conditions of the week.

Weekend Review for the 9th March 2002

Sandridge Rovers hopes of a place in the Cherry Red Books sponsored Capital Counties Feeder Leagues’ Trophy were literally blown away ay Pitstone & Ivinghoe. With a strong cross wind blowing for most of the game good football was impossible. Neither side had a decent attempt on goal during the whole 120 minutes of normal and extra time stalemate. For the first 90 minutes Rovers had marginally the better possession and positional superiority. That situation switched around in extra time where the home side looked the more likely to break the deadlock. Penalties duly arrived and both sides had the second of their allotted five saved. On to sudden death and Rovers fired theirs way over the bar whilst Pitstone kept their cool and slotted home for their third successive penalty shootout victory in the competition.

Little movement in the League as all the main contenders won – with the exception of Oxhey Jets that is. Bedmond Sports remain top of the Premier Division with a 1-0 result at Chipperfield Corinthians. Sun Postal Sports got back on track with a 6-3 home win over relegation threatened Cuffley. London Lions remain third thanks to a 1-0 home win against Metropolitan Police Bushey. Bovingdon are now fourth as they thumped Jekyll and Hyde club Elliott Star 5-0 at Pursley. St Peters pulled off the surprise result of the day winning 2-1 at Oxhey Jets to move seven points clear of the relegation zone. The basement battle between Hatfield Town and Croxley Guild was abandoned after 15 minutes due to high winds at Birchwood.

In Division One Bushey Rangers hold on to top spot thanks to a 2-0 home win against The Cheshunt Club whose own promotion hopes are fading fast. Hadley remain second following a 1-0 home win over Standon & Puckeridge. Old Parmiterians strengthened their grip on third spot with a 3-0 home win over lowly Welwyn. Newcomers Lemsford finished their first League campaign with a 5-0 victory at Benington and currently they occupy fourth spot. North Mymms were thumped 5-0 at home by fast improving Kimpton Rovers for whom the end of the season seems to be coming too soon. In the final game of the afternoon bottom placed Sarratt were humbled 7-0 by Evergreen.

Weekend Review for the 2nd March 2002

A tremendous weekend for Borehamwood based Elliott Star. Despite struggling against relegation in the Premier Division, they dumped Spartan-South Midlands League leaders Greenacres Hemel out of the Herts Senior Centenary Trophy to ensure an all County League final in early April. The 2-1 setback was only the Hemel Hempstead sides second defeat all season.

In the Aubrey Cup Quarter Final, Oxhey Jets kept alive their hopes of a second Cup Final appearance this term with a 3-0 victory at Division One promotion hopefuls Hadley. They’ll now play Sandridge Rovers in the Semi Finals later this month.

In the Premier Division the top match of the day saw morning leaders Sun Postal Sports visit second placed Bedmond Sports. By the end of the afternoon the two teams had exchanged places thanks to Bedmond’s 2-1 success. London Lions and Bovingdon failed to make up any ground. Lions lost 1-0 in the home match against Wormley Rovers – the match switched to Wormley due to the condition of the Copthall pitch. Meanwhile Bovingdon lost 2-1 at home to relegation threatened St Peters. Sandridge Rovers warmed up for their Cherry Red Trophy semi final next week with a 5-0 thrashing of bottom side Hatfield Town at Birchwood. In the final Premier Division game of the day, Metropolitan Police Bushey ended a disastrous run with a 2-0 victory at Cuffley. The result lifts the Police out of the bottom three.

Bushey Rangers took over top spot in Division One, a 4-0 win at Welwyn coupled with Hadley’s league inactivity giving Rangers a one point lead having played two games more. Old Parmiterians moved into third spot with a 3-1 success at Kimpton Rovers. They now lead Lemsford by three points following the newcomers 7-1 drubbing of The Cheshunt Club. The result sees Cheshunt drop from third to fifth – on goal difference. North Mymms strengthened their hold on sixth place with a 2-0 home win over Evergreen.

Finally, worthy of a mention, the League’s last 100% record for the season fell. Old Parmiterians Reserves beat London Lions Reserves in a thrilling match by 3-2. The disappointment for Lions that not only did their record go but Parmiterians took over leadership of the division. However, the Londoners do have three games in hand.

 Weekend Review for the 12th January 2002

 A great day in the Herts Senior Centenary Trophy for County League sides with both Elliott Star and Oxhey Jets knocking out rival league
 opposition. Elliott Star had by far the toughest task on paper as they faced the daunting prospect of visiting the reigning champions Tring Athletic
 who hadn’t lost a tie in the competition for three seasons. Last weekends humiliating defeat at Eton Manor in the Cherry Red Books Trophy was
 hardly the ideal warm up. So a 3-1 victory must rank as the highlight of the Quarter Finals. Oxhey Jets prospects against Leverstock Green seemed
 better. The visitors occupied ninth place (out of twenty) in Spartan-South Midlands Division One and would provide a good test of Jets prospects
 if they succeed in winning promotion to the same league at the end of the season. Leverstock took the lead in the opening 15 minutes and had
 the better of the exchanges in the opening half hour. Steadily Jets got back into the game but as the second half wore on it looked increasingly as
 if the visitor’s solitary goal would be enough. Then with around five minutes left a Leverstock defender handled on the line and Hallum converted
 the penalty, his shot hitting the post on its way in. The second period of extra time saw the most decisive break of the entire match and Page
 coolly slotted past the visitors’ keeper to dump Leverstock out of the competition. The third tie that featured County League sides was Sun Postal
 Sports v Bovingdon. A close match saw Sun join Elliott and Oxhey in the Semi-Finals.

 In the League, London Lions were unable to take advantage of the rest of the top five’s inactivity being held 2-2 at Sandridge Rovers. Wormley
 Rovers moved themselves to the top of the group of nine fighting to avoid the drop spots with a 2-0 success at bottom club Hatfield Town. Seven
 defeats in seven home League games have shackled Town to the bottom and they need a dramatic turnaround in form if they are to avoid an
 instant return to Division One. Chipperfield Corinthians improved their prospects with a 5-0 home win against Croxley Guild. The result leaves
 Croxley precariously placed just above Hatfield. After their backroom upheavals Cuffley are now making a real fight to hold onto their Premier
 Division spot and a 4-0 victory over St Peters did them no harm at all. They’re still third bottom but only a point adrift of a group of three clubs.

 In Division One none of the top three were in League action. Hadley’s game was postponed whilst Bushey Rangers were involved in cup action and
 Old Parmiterians had a weekend off. The Cheshunt Club took advantage to close the gap to just three points following a 4-0 away win at Standon
 & Puckeridge. Lemsford hold fifth spot thanks to a 1-0 win at Welwyn and Mill End Sports are sixth by virtue of a 3-1 home win against North
 Mymms. In the battle of the bottom two Benington took the honours with a 3-2 success at Sarratt. The result lifts Benington above Welwyn and
 leaves Sarratt five point adrift.

 Bushey Rangers finally got to play their Herts Intermediate Cup tie at Flamstead at the fourth attempt and for the County League side the wait
 was worthwhile. Rangers ran out 3-1 victors and will now entertain Oxhey Jets Reserves in next weekends Quarter Final.

 Weekend Review for the 5th January 2002

 The Friday night/Saturday morning thaw came just too late to save half of the League’s first programme of 2002. However, quite a number of
 games did survive and at the top of the Premier Division Sun Postal Sports opened up a three point lead with a 2-1 home win against Chipperfield
 Corinthians. Fifth placed Bovingdon took advantage of others inactivity to close on the top group thanks to a 2-1 home win over rock bottom
 Hatfield Town. In the remaining Premier Division game Sandridge Rovers beat Wormley Rovers 4-1 for three very valuable away points. The result
 lifts Sandridge from eleventh to sixth place.

 The long overdue Cherry Red Books sponsored Capital Counties Feeder Leagues’ Trophy Third Round tie between Eton Manor and Elliott Star finally

 In Division One The Cheshunt Club moved up into fourth place with a 5-1 vicory over struggling Sarratt. The remaining game ended 1-1 between

 Weekend Review for the 22nd December 2001

 The weekend before Christmas and the weather bites hard for the first time this season. Only two games off in the Premier Division – and credit to
 those clubs who made the effort to get their pitches playable. Division One though only had one survivor.

 No change at the top of the Premier Division despite the top four all being in action and each having varying fortune. The big game of the day was
 undoubtedly Oxhey Jets v Sun Postal Sports. Sun having reached the top last weekend were anxious to hold on to it whilst Oxhey as reigning
 Champions needed to do a "Manchester United" and prove they don’t plan to give up their title easily. The final result, 2-0 to Oxhey Jets. They
 remain in fourth spot and the Sun remain top. However the Watford’s sides lead is now only on goal difference as London Lions drew 2-2 at home
 against Chipperfield Corinthians. That’s eight draws in sixteen League matches for Corinthians the League’s draw specialists. Bedmond retained
 third spot but closed the gap to just two points following a 2-1 win at Wormley Rovers. The final Premier Division game saw Sandridge Rovers beat
 Cuffley 4-1 at home to ease their own relegation fears but set back Cuffley’s recent revival.
 The only game in Division One was at Herts Police HQ where Welwyn lost 1-0 at home in a hard fought contest to Evergreen.

 The Herts Intermediate Cup tie between Flamstead and Bushey Rangers was postponed for a second successive week and will now be
 re-scheduled for Saturday 29th December.
 The rest of the League’s clubs will have a weekend off as the weather has so far been kind and League action will recommence on Saturday 5th
 January 2002.

 Weekend Review for the 15th December 2001

 Sun Postal Sports took full advantage of London Lions inactivity to take over at the top of the Premier Division. Their 2-1 victory at Wormley
 Rovers lifts them a point clear of Lions at the top. Bedmond maintained their challenge with a 1-0 home victory over improving Elliott Star. The
 result lifts Bedmond to third. Reigning champions Oxhey Jets moved up to fourth with a 4-2 win at last season’s landlords Metropolitan Police
 Bushey. The divisions two remaining games between Chipperfield and St Peters and Hatfield Town and Croxley Guild were postponed.

 In the Cherry Red Books sponsored Capital Counties Feeder Leagues’ Trophy, Sandridge Rovers ensured the League will have a representative in
 the semi finals thanks to a 2-1 win at Frenford Senior. The result gives Rovers a home tie against Oxhey Jets in the quarter finals in January.

 In Division One Old Parmiterians missed out on the opportunity to close the gap on Hadley and Bushey Rangers, only able to draw 3-3 at North
 Mymms. Mill End’s 2-2 home draw with Lemsford ensured they hold on to fourth place albeit on goal difference from Kimpton Rovers who beat
 Benington 2-1 at home. Having tasted victory at home for the first time last week, Standon & Puckeridge followed up with their second home
 maximum points haul with a 2-0 win against bottom club Sarratt.

 The League have three representatives in the last eight of the Herts Intermediate Cup – with a fourth possible. Hadley won 2-0 at London Colney
 Reserves. Oxhey Jets Reserves overcame Welwyn Garden City Reserves 2-1 at home whilst London Lions Reserves beat Welwyn 2-1. Bushey
 Rangers may yet join this trio. Their tie at Flamstead was postponed.

 In the Reserve Cup Hadley beat St Peters 4-0 to complete the quarter final line up.

 Weekend Review for the 8th December 2001

 Congratulations to Oxhey Jets. They travelled to Sheerwater and triumphed 2-1 in the third round of the Cherry Red Books sponsored Capital
 County Feeder Leagues’ Trophy. At present they are the League’s only representatives in the last eight although Sandridge Rovers play next
 weekend and Elliott Star on 5th January. Oxhey’s reward is a visit to the winners of the Frenford Senior/Sandridge Rovers tie. Croxley Guild bowed
 out 1-0 after extra time at home against South Midlands League Stony Stratford Town whilst Bovingdon were well beaten, 5-2, at Essex side
 Epping.

 In the League their was a major Premier Division shock at Copthall where top placed London Lions were humbled by bottom of the table visitors
 Cuffley, the final score 2-1. The result lifts Cuffley out of bottom place whilst London Lions lead at the top was trimmed to two points following
 Sun Postal Sports 3-1 home win against Sandridge Rovers. Bedmond Sports moved back into contention with a 2-0 home success over Chipperfield
 Corinthians. St Peters and Elliott Star shared the points in a 1-1 draw at Toulmin Drive to maintain station in the division whilst in the remaining
 game Wormley Rovers recorded a 2-1 home victory against Metropolitan Police Bushey.

 In Division One the top three are pulling away from the rest with an eight point gap now developed between third and fourth. Old Parmiterians and
 Lemsford respectively hold the positions and when they met the Garston based club recorded a 1-0 away victory. Hadley ended Welwyn’s recent
 improvement with a 2-1 away victory to stay five points clear at the top whilst Bushey Rangers maintained their hold on second spot with a 3-1
 win at Sarratt. There were goals galore in the divisions three remaining games. The Cheshunt Club lost at home 6-5 to Mill End Sports; North
 Mymms recorded their second home win with a 6-2 thumping of Benington whilst Standon & Puckeridge recorded their first home win of the season
 in beating Kimpton Rovers 5-3.

 The Reserve Cup saw holders Hatfield Town beaten 2-0 by Oxhey Jets. Bedmond, Elliott Star, Bovingdon, Old Parmiterians, London Lions and
 Wormley Rovers join Oxhey in the last eight with Hadley and St Peters playing for the final spot next weekend.

 Weekend Review for the 1st December 2001

 Away the lads - not quite Geordie - but it was success all the way for the away sides in the League this weekend.
 In the Premier Division, the match of the day saw leaders London Lions beat second placed reigning champions Oxhey Jets 2-1.
 Sun Postal Sports enhanced their chances of winning the title with a 1-0 win at Chipperfield Corinthians. Similarly Bovingdon kept up their
 challenge with a 2-0 win at fellow title hopefuls Bedmond.
 At the other end of the table St Peters eased their worries with a 7-1 victory against basement club Cuffley. Rapidly improving Elliott Star
 triumphed 4-1 at Hatfield Town who retain their unwanted 100% home record – beaten in every game at Birchwood this season.
 The matches between Croxley Guild and Wormley Rovers and Sandridge Rovers and Metropolitan Police Bushey were both postponed due to
 waterlogged pitches.

 In Division One Hadley kept up their relentless pursuit of the title with a 2-1 victory at Lemsford. Welwyn kept up their recent improvement with a
 4-3 win at Kimpton Rovers whilst Benington earned a rare victory with a 3-1 success at Mill End.
 The days remaining Division One games, between Bushey Rangers and The Cheshunt Club; North Mymms and Evergreen; and Sarratt and Old
 Parmiterians; were all victims to the weather.

 Weekend Review for the 24th November 2001

 Half the Quarter Finalists in the Herts Senior Centenary Trophy will be from the County League. Bovingdon had perhaps the best result of the day
 coming back from 2-1 down at half time to win 4-2 at former County League club Kings Langley. Oxhey Jets progressed thanks to a 4-0 home win
 over Hatfield Town whilst Sun Postal Sports and Elliott Star recorded away victories at Metropolitan Police (4-1) and St Peters (5-2) respectively.
 Sandridge Rovers and Wormley Rovers both made Cup exits although they did push their opponents all the way. Sandridge lost 6-5 on penalties
 following a 3-3 draw at the end of normal and extra time at Colney Heath and Wormley bowed out 6-3 after extra time at home against Leverstock
 Green.

 In the League, London Lions established a 5 point lead at the top of the Premier Division with a 1-0 win over Bedmond at Copthall. The days other
 Premier game saw Chipperfield ease their relegation fears with a 2-1 success against bottom club Cuffley.
 Hadley continue to top Division One but in the top v bottom clash they scraped home 2-1 against Sarratt. Bushey Rangers kept pace with a 4-1
 win at home against Mill End. Old Parmiterians 3-2 home win ensured they remain in third spot whilst The Cheshunt Club's 2-1 result against
 Welwyn improves their prospects of a top three finish. Evergreen recorded a rare home victory beating Standon & Puckeridge 3-0 whilst in the
 afternoon's remaining game North Mymms picked up three away points with a 3-0 victory at Benington.

 Weekend Review for the 17th November 2001

 Croxley Guild became the League's fourth team to qualify for the 3rd round of the Cherry Red Books sponsored Capital Counties Feeder Leagues'
 Trophy with a 1-0 win at Combined Counties League side Hartley Wintney. Make no mistake, this was a tremendous result against a side currently
 second in their league which is classified one step above the Herts Senior County Premier. Guild will now be the League's only home side in the
 next round as they entertain Spartan-South Midlands side Stony Stratford Town.
 In the Premier Division, London Lions returned to the top with a 2-1 victory at fellow newcomers St Peters. Reigning champions Oxhey Jets closed
 to second with a 3-1 win at Hatfield Town who continue to occupy a relegation spot. In the game of the day, Sun Postal Sports moved into third
 with a 4-0 thrashing of Bedmond who drop to fifth. The mornings leaders, Bovingdon, didn't have a game and drop to fourth. At the foot of the
 table Elliott Star climber off the bottom and up two places thanks to a 5-2 victory at Sandridge Rovers. Cuffley now occupy last place following
 their 2-0 setback at Metropolitan Police Bushey - they do however have games in hand. The days other Premier Division game ended goalless
 between Chipperfield and Wormley Rovers.
 Division One's only game saw Kimpton Rovers beat basement club Sarratt 3-1 to climb up to fifth spot.

 In the Herts Intermediate Cup Second Round Bushey Rangers beat Benington 3-0; Hadley beat Mill End Sports 6-5 on penalties after the game
 ended 1-1; Welwyn beat Potters Bar Reserves 2-1; The Cheshunt Club beat Harpenden Town Reserves 6-3; and Evergreen won at Elliott Star
 Reserves 6-0. London Lions Reserves pulled off a shock result beating North Mymms 4-0whilst Oxhey Jets Reserves overcame Hatfield Town
 Reserves 2-1. No progress though for Standon & Puckeridge - beaten 5-4 at Hertford Town Reserves; Old Parmiterians - beaten 2-1 at holders
 Bishops Stortford Swifts; Bovingdon Reserves - beaten 1-0 at London Colney Reserves; and Chipperfield Corinthians Reserves - beaten 6-1 at
 Flamstead.

 Weekend Review for the 10th November 2001

 Cherry Red Trophy day proved to be disappointing in general for County League clubs with more bowing out than progressing to the last sixteen.
 Three clubs were successful though - and all away from home.
 Elliott Star, not enjoying good form in the League, won 4-3 after extra time at Middlesex side Spelthorne Sports - their reward is a trip to Essex
 based Eton Manor in round 3. The Manor progressed thanks to a 3-2 victory against County League Hatfield Town. So far "home" is proving to be a
 disadvantage for Town who have lost all four home League matches as well.
 Bovingdon, the Premier Division leaders comfortably dispatched Farleigh Rovers 5-1 away and will now visit Epping in round 3. Epping were
 responsible for the exit of another County League outfit, Metropolitan Police Bushey who went down 2-0 on their travels.
 Sandridge Rovers pulled off perhaps the best result of the day with a 3-1 win at last years runners-up, Virginia Water. They'll now travel to
 Frenford Senior.
 Oxhey Jets progressed to a third round tie away at Sheerwater without even kicking a ball. Their opponents Barnston withdrew as they couldn't
 raise a team to travel to the "early" kick off!
 Wormley Rovers bowed out 4-1 at White Notley, Chipperfield lost 2-0 at home against North Greenford United, London Lions lost out 5-2 at
 Copthall against Pitstone & Ivinghoe, and St Peters were beaten 4-2 at home by Sony Stratford Town.
 Croxley Guild will now meet Hartley Wintney this coming weekend.

 In the League Bedmond finally got back to winning ways with a 6-2 mauling of Croxley Guild. The result lifts Bedmond to 3rd, one point behind
 leaders Bovingdon. Indeed, only two points separate the first five clubs in the Premier Division and only four points separate the bottom nine!
 In Division One, Hadley maintained top spot with a hard fought 3-2 home win against The Cheshunt Club. Bushey Rangers kept the pressure on
 with a 3-1 win at Kimpton Rovers. Lemsford missed out on the opportunity to go third as they were held 2-2 at home by North Mymms. Mill End
 move up to fifth following a 5-0 thrashing of Sarratt who drop to the bottom of the table. Welwyn's second win of the season, 3-2 at home to
 Standon & Puckeridge, lifts them two places off the bottom. The afternoons remaining game between Benington and Evergreen saw six goals and
 the points shared at 3-3.

 Weekend Review for 3rd November 2001

 Aubrey Cup 2nd Round day saw one shock and also - in line with recent tradition - the holders making their exit. The shock result came at Croxley
 where the Guild were thrashed at home by Division One outfit Bushey Rangers - the final score 6-2. Holders Elliott Star tumbled 3-2 at home
 against fellow Premier Division side Sandridge Rovers. Runners up Hatfield Town scraped through 3-2 on penalties after the match ended 3-3
 following extra time. Bedmond completed the most miserable of hattricks, going down 3-1 to reigning Premier Division champions Oxhey Jets -
 that's three cup competition exits in three weeks. Sun Postal Sports got back into winning ways and showed the gulf in class between the top end
 of the Premier Division and the middle of Division One trouncing Kimpton Rovers on their own patch 8-0. Division One leaders Hadley joined Bushey
 Rangers in the Quarter Finals with a 7-0 demolishion of Sarratt. Cuffley took local rivals Wormley Rovers to extra time but crumbled in the extra
 period to lose 4-1 whilst in the final tie of the afternoon Metropolitan Police Bushey comfortably beat Division One outfit North Mymms 5-2 to book
 their place in the last eight. The Quarter Finals line up as follows:- Sandridge Rovers v Hatfield Town; Sun Postal Sports v Wormley Rovers; Bushey
 Rangers v Met Police Bushey; and Hadley v Oxhey Jets.
 In the League, Bovingdon went top thanks to a 7-0 win at St Peters in the afternoon's only Premier Division encounter.
 Three games were played in Division One. Lemsford moved up to fourth following a 1-0 home win against Benington whilst Old Parmiterians
 improved their promotion prospects with a 1-0 win at Evergreen and Mill End recorded a 4-1 away success at Standon & Puckeridge. The
 afternoon's other scheduled game between The Cheshunt Club and Welwyn had to be called off when demands on the Hertfordshire constabulary
 took away the majority of the Welwyn side.

 Weekend Review for the 27th October 2001

 Shock result of the day was Hatfield Town's 3-1 victory at Sun Postal Sports. Sun had the opportunity of returning to the top of the Premier
 Division and on paper looked certs to pick up three points against a struggling Hatfield outfit. It didn't turn out that way though and Town returned
 home with a much needed victory. Oxhey Jets might also have deposed London Lions at the top but a 3-2 defeat at Bovingdon put paid to their
 hopes. So Lions remain top despite being in Herts Centenary Trophy action. Not a happy afternoon for them though, as St peters - visitors to
 Copthall Stadium held them to 1-1 at full time and 3-3 at the end of extra time and them dumped them out of the Trophy 8-7 on penalties.
 Elsewhere in the Premier Division, Elliott Star earned a much needed home three points with a 2-1 win against Wormley Rovers. Meanwhile in the
 Centenary Trophy Chipperfield lost 4-0 at home against former County Leaguers Kings Langley and Cuffley lost 3-2 at home to another former
 County League side, Colney Heath. Met Police beat fellow County League side Bedmond 2-1 - Bedmond's second cup exit in eight days - and
 Sandridge Rovers hammered Croxley Guild 6-0. In Division One two games fell victim to Friday nights rain - Hadley v Evergreen and Sarratt v
 Benington. In the Division's game of the day Bushey Rangers took advantage of Hadley's inactivity to close the gap at the top to two points with a
 3-2 win at third placed Old Parmiterians. Mill End and Kimpton Rovers shared eight goals in the highest scoring senior game of the day and Standon
 & Puckeridge and Lemsford also shared the points with two goals a piece. Congratulations to Welwyn. They beat North Mymms 2-0 to record their
 first League victory of the season at the eleventh attempt. They are now just a point adrift of Sarratt and Benington at the foot of the table.

 Weekend Review - Saturday 20th October 2001

 Sun Postal Sports encountered their worst afternoon of the season. A 2-0 defeat in the Cherry Red Books Trophy at Netherne Village has cost
 them a bumper pay day. The winners of the tie host Essex Senior League leaders Enfield Town in the next round. Enfield, formed by a group of
 "true" Enfield supporters have regularly been drawing crowds of 300-400 for away games. To compound Sun's misery they lost top spot in the
 Premier Division as London Lions recovered from last weekends set back to beat newboys St Peters 3-1 at Copthall. Also making early exits from
 the Cherry Red competition were Cuffley, mauled 8-1 at home by current holders Tring Athletic, and Bedmond, the 1999-2000 finalists beaten 2-1
 at home by Frenford Senior. Wormley Rovers carry the League flag into the next round though following their 3-0 victory at Spartan-South
 Midlands League Winslow United. In the League, Oxhey Jets closed in on the top two with a 4-2 home victory over struggling Hatfield Town. There
 were also victories for Croxley Guild, Bovingdon and Met Police. In Division One Hadley extended their lead with a 2-0 home result against
 basement club Welwyn. Evergreen and North Mymms shared the points in a goalless draw. In the Herts Intermediate Cup, Senior sides Lemsford,
 Benington, Bushey Rangers, The Cheshunt Club, Mill End Sports and Old Parmiterians all progressed to the second round whilst Kimpton Rovers and
 Sarratt were first round casualties.
